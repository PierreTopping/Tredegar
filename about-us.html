History of the Project

The earliest origins of this project date back to 1991, operating as a localized BBS (Bulletin Board System) and utilizing academic gateways via the JANET network to share transcription files and local census indices. Around 1999/2000, the infrastructure migrated onto the World Wide Web as WWW.TREDEGAR.CO.UK, expanding into vibrant community forums and holding over 10,000 archival images before suspending operations due to historical server and hosting overheads.

Expanded Digital Research Archive

The platform is now being completely re-designed and re-structured as a lightweight repository to house the original digital database alongside a massive collection of newly discovered records, cartographic assets, and family histories gathered in the years since.

Looking for deeper records? Thousands of fully digitized historic documents, rare town photographs, academic white papers, industrial charts, and vintage maps are preserved within our secure back-end environment.
